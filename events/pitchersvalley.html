Pitchers' Valley is the prominent event of the Pune StartUp Fest, where startups get the opportunity to showcase their innovative ideas in front of top investors.

Details

  • Eligibility: All registered startups.

  • Date and Time :
    • Stage 1: Results will be out on 9 March
    • Stage 2: Results will be out on 11 March
    • The Pitcher's Valley: 12th March

  • Venue: COEP Tech
  • Last date to register: 4th March

Structure

  • The event features a three-minute live pitch from the participating startups, offering the potential to win investment and valuable feedback from experienced investors in the industry.
  • To participate, startups need to register in advance and prepare a compelling pitch deck that showcases their product or service, value proposition, and growth potential.
  • Stage 1: Among the registered startups, the top 50 will be selected.
  • Stage 2: In the next stage, from detailed scrutiny 10 best startups and 2 exclusive student startups will get an opportunity to pitch.
  • With five strong investors present to hear their pitch, this event is a must-attend for any startup looking to take their business to the next level.
  • The "Pitchers' Valley" event is scheduled to take place on March 12, 2023, at the COEP Tech.
  • Don't miss out on this incredible opportunity to make your startup dreams a reality.

Judging Criteria

  • Startup will be evaluated on the following judging criteria:
  • Product & Business Description:
    Details of the venture and what it does.
    How well was the concept explained?
    How reasonable, sustainable, and scalable is the new concept?
  • Market Analysis & Traction:
    Characteristics of the market and description of its customers.
    Is there a genuine need for the product or service?
    How well was the target market defined?
    What is the size and growth of the market?
    What is the consumers' willingness to pay for the product/service?
  • Marketing Strategy:
    How sales will be achieved?
    How realistically defined is the marketing plan?
    Does the plan adequately address price, product, place, and promotion?
    Are resources sufficiently allocated for marketing?
  • Team:
    An assessment of the entrepreneur(s) and team.
    Does the team exhibit the experience and skills required for operation?
    What is the depth and breadth of the team's capabilities?
  • Presentation:
    Overall effectiveness of the actual presentation.
    Did the presenter(s) engage the audience and hold their attention?
    Did the presenter(s) appear to speak with confidence authority?
    Were visual aids (i.e,PowerPointÂ® slides) clear and valuable?
    Was the pitch exciting and compelling?
    How efficiently did the team allot their time.
